🌳 IHT Readiness Decision Tree (Post-2022 Rules)
STEP 0: Do you have estate data?
If no → Block flow. Prompt to complete estate tab.

If yes → Use:

Gross value of estate (before liabilities)

Net value of estate (after liabilities)

STEP 1: Was the deceased domiciled in the UK?
❌ No → Not excepted → Must submit IHT400 + IHT421

✅ Yes → Continue

STEP 2: Does gross estate value exceed £325,000?
❌ No → Continue

✅ Yes → Proceed to Step 3 (check for threshold extensions)

STEP 3: Are threshold extensions available?
3.1 Was the deceased married or in a civil partnership at death?
✅ Yes → Spouse exemption may apply → Likely no tax on spousal inheritance

❌ No → Check TNRB (next)

3.2 Had their spouse/partner already died?
✅ Yes → Continue

❌ No → Cannot use TNRB → May exceed threshold → IHT400 likely

3.3 Did the spouse use all their NRB?
✅ Yes → No transfer → Proceed to Step 4

❌ No → Add remaining allowance to threshold (max total: £650,000)

3.4 Did deceased leave home to children or grandchildren?
✅ Yes → Add Residence Nil Rate Band (RNRB) (up to £175,000)

❌ No → No RNRB added

➡️ Calculate total threshold = £325,000 + any TNRB + RNRB
➡️ If gross estate > total threshold → Not excepted → IHT400 required

STEP 4: Are there any disqualifying features?
If any of these are true, the estate is not excepted and IHT400 is required:

Question	Trigger
Gifts made in last 7 years over £3,000/year?	✅
Assets held overseas?	✅
Beneficiary of a trust?	✅

STEP 5: Final Decision
Outcome	Condition
✅ Excepted Estate	Deceased domiciled in UK, under threshold (incl. TNRB/RNRB), no disqualifying factors
❌ Not Excepted	Any of: Non-UK domicile, over threshold with no extensions, gifts/trusts/foreign assets

👇 What To Do Next
Outcome	Action
✅ Excepted	Skip IHT form submission; advise to retain records; allow PA1P to proceed
❌ Not Excepted	Block PA1P until user confirms:

IHT400 submitted to HMRC

IHT421 sent to HMCTS
