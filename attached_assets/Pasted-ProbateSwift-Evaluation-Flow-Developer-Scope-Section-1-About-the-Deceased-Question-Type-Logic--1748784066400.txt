ProbateSwift Evaluation Flow – Developer Scope
🔹 Section 1: About the Deceased
Question	Type	Logic	Description (user-facing)
Was the deceased domiciled in the UK at death?	Boolean	If “No” → Exit; ProbateSwift cannot assist	“Domicile” means the country the person considered their permanent home, even if they died elsewhere.
Did the deceased live permanently in England or Wales?	Boolean	If “No” → Flag for review	Did they live most of their life and plan to stay in England or Wales?
Did the deceased own non-UK (foreign) assets?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Flag; may require extra forms (IHT400)	Did they own property, money, or investments outside the UK?
Did the deceased own land held as settled land?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Flag for specialist review	Settled land is a legal term for certain types of land trusts (rare—leave blank if unsure).
Did the deceased hold assets under another name?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Collect aliases for grant	Did the person have any bank accounts, property, or investments in any other names (including maiden names, aliases, or nicknames)?
Were any relatives adopted in/out of the family?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Flag for review	Was anyone adopted into or out of the deceased’s family (could affect inheritance rules)?

🔹 Section 2: Tax & Estate Threshold
Question	Type	Logic	Description (user-facing)
Did the deceased make gifts in the 7 years before death?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Estate is not excepted, IHT400 needed	Did they give away money, property, or valuable items in the last 7 years of life?
Was the deceased married or in a civil partnership at death?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Enable spousal exemption logic	Were they married or in a civil partnership when they died?
Had their spouse/civil partner already died?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Enable transferable nil rate band logic	Had their spouse or partner died before them?
Was the spouse’s nil rate band fully used?	Boolean	If “No” → Add unused portion to current threshold	Did the spouse/partner use all their inheritance tax allowance, or is some available to transfer? (If unsure, select “Not sure.”)
Did the deceased leave their home to children or grandchildren?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Enable residence nil rate band logic	Was the family home left to children or grandchildren?
Did the deceased live in a UK property they owned?	Boolean	If “Yes” → RNRB logic applies	Did they live in a house or flat in the UK that they owned?
Did the deceased create or benefit from a trust?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Estate is not excepted, IHT400 needed	Did they put assets into a trust or benefit from a trust at any point? (If unsure, select “No.”)

🔹 Section 3: Will & Executors
(Show only if “there is a will” / PA1P flow)

Question	Type	Logic	Description (user-facing)
What is the date of the will?	Date	Always required for PA1P	Enter the date shown on the will.
Did the deceased marry after making the will?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Will may be revoked; flag for review	Did the deceased get married or register a civil partnership after their last will was made? (This may affect validity.)
Are there codicils to the will?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Originals must be provided	Are there any official changes or addendums (codicils) attached to the will?
Were any wills made outside England and Wales?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Flag for translation/legal review	Did the deceased make any wills in another country? (Copies may be needed.)
Are all named executors applying?	Boolean	If “No” → Must explain why for each non-applying executor	Are all the executors listed in the will actually applying for probate?
Why is each non-applying executor not applying?	Select (renunciation, deceased, cannot locate, lacks capacity, power reserved)	Show for each non-applying executor	Please state the reason for each executor not applying (choose from the list).

🔹 Section 4: About You (Applicant)
Question	Type	Logic	Description (user-facing)
Are you named as an executor in the will?	Boolean	If “No” → Check if applying under power of attorney	Are you personally named as an executor in the will?
Are you acting under a power of attorney on behalf of an executor?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Proceed; else, ineligible	Are you applying as a legally appointed representative for a named executor?
How many people are applying?	Number (1–4)	Must be at least 1, max 4	How many people will be named as applicants on this probate application?
Is anyone under 18 receiving a gift in the will?	Boolean	If “Yes” → Require at least 2 applicants	Will any beneficiaries under 18 receive money or property from the estate? (If yes, at least two applicants must apply.)

🔹 Section 5: Inheritance Tax Readiness
Question	Type	Logic	Description (user-facing)
Is the estate excepted from Inheritance Tax?	Boolean	If “Yes” → No IHT400 needed; proceed with PA1P or PA1A	Is the total estate below the inheritance tax threshold or otherwise exempt? (Most simple estates are excepted.)
If not excepted, has an IHT400 been completed and submitted?	Boolean	If “No” → Block PA1P/PA1A; must submit IHT400 first	If the estate isn’t excepted, has the Inheritance Tax form (IHT400) already been completed and sent to HMRC?
