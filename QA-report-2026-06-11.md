# ProbateSwift QA pass — 11 June 2026

Tester: Claude (acting as customer/QA), logged in as David Barnes (david@02n.ltd).
Scope: live site walkthrough, full assessment, supported scenario, unsupported scenario.

## Headline

The product *works* — the supported journey (assessment → "probate required" → case created → milestone tasks) and the unsupported branch both behave correctly. But they only work on a dashboard the live site never sends users to, and several entry points are broken. As it stands, a real visitor typing your domain and signing up would hit a parked page, a dead button, or a white screen before reaching the working app.

## Critical (fix before any traffic)

**1. Apex domain serves a GoDaddy parked page.**
`https://probateswift.com` (no www) redirects to `/lander` — the GoDaddy "this domain is parked" page with ads. Only `https://www.probateswift.com` serves the app. Anyone typing the domain, or any link/share that drops the `www`, lands on parking. This is a DNS/redirect misconfiguration (apex not pointing at the app, or no apex→www 301). Single biggest leak.

**2. The default post-login dashboard is broken; the working one is unlinked.**
After login the app lands on `/dashboard`. There, "Start Assessment" is dead — the onClick fires but nothing happens (no navigation, no network call, no error). The "Evaluation" tab dead-ends: "Create a Probate Case First → Go to Overview", but Overview's button is the dead one. So a signed-up user has no way forward.
The *working* dashboard is `/new-dashboard` (richer UI, working assessment modal, tasks, progress). Nothing links to it. Users never reach it on their own. Either point auth at `/new-dashboard` and retire `/dashboard`, or port the working assessment trigger onto `/dashboard`.

**3. `/estate` white-screens.**
The Estate page renders blank and throws an uncaught `TypeError: Cannot read properties of null (reading 'length')`. Estate valuation is a headline feature and a required step in the flow. Looks like a null-vs-empty-array on a case with no estate data yet (reproduced on a freshly created case).

## High

**4. `/assessment` 404s with a developer message.**
Direct visit to `/assessment` shows "404 Page Not Found — Did you forget to add the page to the router?". That dev-facing string should never reach a customer, and the route arguably should exist given the marketing nav has an "Assessment" item.

**5. Marketing CTAs don't go to the assessment.**
Hero "Start for Free" and "Start your probate assessment" both route to `/auth` (login), not the assessment. The homepage "Begin Assessment" did not open anything on first load. Worth a clean logged-out pass to confirm the public funnel (visitor → assessment → register) actually connects — I couldn't test it because the browser session was already logged in as you (and I can't re-enter a password to log back in if I sign out). Happy to run it if you log out first.

## Medium / polish

**6. Duplicate button in hero.** "Is probate required?" renders twice, stacked, in the hero.

**7. Old dashboard profile shows placeholders.** `/dashboard` profile card shows "Account Created: N/A" and "Last Login: N/A".

**8. Aggressive rate limiting.** Normal polling plus a couple of actions tripped `429 Too Many Requests` across `/api/*`, which stalled the "Creating case..." step mid-flow until it cleared. Worth checking the limit threshold and that the UI handles 429 gracefully rather than hanging.

**9. Unsupported-branch copy mismatch.** Answering "no" to "has the person passed away?" shows the heading "Probate May Not Be Required". The actual reason is that the person is alive (out of scope), not a probate-requirement determination. Heading and body contradict each other. Suggest a heading like "This isn't something we can help with yet".

**10. "When probate may be granted" looks hardcoded.** The estimate showed 11 December 2026 — exactly six months from today. If it's a flat "+6 months" heuristic, presenting it as a specific date may overpromise.

## Scenario results

**Scenario A — supported, progresses into the app: PASS.**
Assessment answers (person has passed away: yes; valid will: yes) → "Based on your answers, probate is required" → case created → dashboard updates to "you're applying for probate with a will", progress 15%, and milestone tasks unlock: Advanced Evaluation, Enter Executor Info, Upload Death Certificate, Upload Will. Clean. (Note: account creation itself wasn't exercised since you were already logged in — see item 5.)

**Scenario B — unsupported, handled gracefully: PASS.**
Answering "no" to "has the person passed away?" stops the flow with a clear modal — "Probate May Not Be Required / We can only assist with probate for someone who has passed away" — and offers Restart Assessment / Return to Dashboard. No dead-end, no forced signup. Good. (Copy nit in item 9.)

## What works well

`/people` (add people, legal professionals) and `/documents` (drag-drop, document categories, status tabs) are polished and render cleanly. The `/new-dashboard` task/milestone model is clear and motivating.

## Couldn't fully verify

- Full assessment question set: the indicator showed six steps, but a supported determination was reached after two answers, so steps 3–6 weren't observed. (Radix radio controls also resisted automation, so this was partly a tooling limit on my side.)
- The public logged-out funnel and the real registration form (session was already authenticated).
