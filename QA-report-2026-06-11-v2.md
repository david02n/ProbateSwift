# ProbateSwift QA pass v2 — 11 June 2026 (redeployed site)

Tester: Claude (acting as customer/QA), logged out (true new-visitor funnel).
Scope: redesigned marketing site, full public assessment, supported scenario through to signup, unsupported scenario.

## Headline

Big step up from the earlier build. The site has been redesigned and the funnel now works the way it should: a clean five-question assessment, an honest ungated result, and the right next step for both supported and unsupported cases. Every critical issue from the first pass is resolved. Nothing blocking found this time — only small copy nits.

## Critical issues from v1 — now fixed

- **Bare domain no longer parked.** `probateswift.com` (no www) now serves the real app, not the GoDaddy parking page.
- **No dead "Start Assessment" button / no broken dashboard split.** The assessment is now a self-contained public flow that works on first click. The old `/dashboard` vs `/new-dashboard` confusion isn't in the visitor path.
- **No leaky `/assessment` 404 or duplicate hero button** on the new homepage.

(These were tested as a logged-out visitor. The logged-in `/estate` white-screen from v1 couldn't be re-checked without an account — worth confirming separately.)

## The assessment (5 questions, public, ungated)

1. Did the person who died leave a valid will? (Yes / No / Not sure)
2. Roughly, what is the estate worth in total? (Under £5k / £5k–£325k / £325k–£2m / Over £2m)
3. Did they own a home or other property? (Sole name / Jointly / No property)
4. How many bank or investment accounts? (1–3 / 4–8 / More than 8)
5. Do any of these apply? (Dispute/contested will / Trusts / Overseas assets / Insolvent / None)

Card-based, one click advances, progress bar, Back and "Save & exit" present. Smooth and fast. The result shows before any signup, as the homepage promises ("ungated result").

## Scenario results

**Scenario A — supported → account creation: PASS.**
Answers: valid will → £5k–£325k → property in sole name → 1–3 accounts → none of the complex flags. Result: "You look like a good fit — Good news, this is exactly what we're built for," with the £295 fee restated and a "Create your free account / Get started free" CTA. That leads to the Clerk-secured `/auth` page (Google or email, with Terms/Privacy/Cookie links). Clean progression. I stopped at the signup screen rather than creating an account.

**Scenario B — unsupported → honest turn-away: PASS.**
Same answers but flagged "a dispute or contested will" at Q5. Result: "Worth a closer look — This estate may need a solicitor," explaining it's safer to use a solicitor, with no signup push and an optional "Keep me posted" email waitlist instead. This is exactly the "turns away the hard cases" promise, handled gracefully. Much better than v1's contradictory "Probate May Not Be Required" screen.

## Smaller things to tidy

1. **Signup page copy mismatch.** Clicking "Create your free account / Get started free" lands on a page headed "Sign in to continue your probate application" and "Welcome back! Please sign in to continue." That's sign-in framing for someone you just invited to *create* an account. There's a "Sign up" link, but the default should probably open in sign-up mode for this entry point.
2. **Stale "Last used" hint.** The Clerk widget showed "Last used" on Continue-with-Google — leftover from a previous session on this browser. A genuinely new visitor shouldn't see it, but worth confirming it isn't shown based on shared/cached state.
3. **Estimated grant time.** Homepage states "~8–12 weeks (current Probate Registry processing time, not us)" — good that it's attributed. Just keep it current.
