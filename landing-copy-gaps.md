# ProbateSwift landing page: copy gap analysis

Based on a review of the four competitors that compete on messaging (Farewill, Octopus Legacy, Co-op Legal, Kings Court Trust) against ProbateSwift's current landing copy. Budget grant-only providers and GOV.UK are covered in the harness and don't add messaging signal.

Ordered by priority. Each area has the gap, what competitors do, and concrete copy to drop in or adapt.

---

## 1. Trust scaffolding (highest priority)

**Gap:** The current page has a Testimonials component but no ratings, review counts, or trust badges in the hero or anywhere a hesitant buyer sees first. For a grief-and-money purchase, "can I trust this?" is the first objection and nothing answers it.

**What competitors do:**
- Farewill: "Over 18,000 reviews, 4.9 on Trustpilot" plus award badges, repeated three times down the page
- Co-op: "trusted to manage over £3 billion in estates each year", "largest team of probate solicitors in England and Wales"
- KCT: "40,000+ families", "ICAEW regulated", "award-winning"
- Octopus: "Rated Excellent on Trustpilot", "SRA-regulated", named solicitor with 15 years' experience

**Suggestions (use whatever is genuinely true today):**
- Add a trust strip directly under the hero CTA: review count or rating if you have one, families/estates helped, "Bank-level encryption", "Built in the UK".
- If review volume is low, lead with a named human and credentials instead: founder, advisory input, or the qualifications behind the form logic.
- Add a Security line and ideally a short Security page (Farewill has one). State: data encrypted, never sold, you control submission.
- Put one strong testimonial near the hero, not only in a lower section.

---

## 2. The "is it safe to do this myself?" fear

**Gap:** The self-serve model triggers an anxiety the copy never addresses: am I exposed if I get it wrong? Co-op turns this into their main weapon.

**What competitors do:**
- Co-op: "estate administration is not a reserved legal activity... anyone can offer these services even if they are not qualified, insured or regulated" and "if mistakes are made during probate, the executor or administrator can be held legally or financially responsible."

**Suggestions:**
- Add a reassurance block answering it head-on. Draft:
  > **Get it right, without a solicitor.** Every figure is checked before you submit. We validate your forms against HMRC and Probate Registry requirements and flag anything that looks wrong, so you apply with confidence, not guesswork.
- Tie this to your readiness-gate / pre-submission validation work from the harness. That feature is your answer to this fear, so name it in the copy.
- Add an honest "When you might still need a solicitor" line (see area 6). Admitting the boundary increases trust in everything else.

---

## 3. IHT forms: your sharpest wedge, currently buried

**Gap:** "We complete your IHT forms" is your strongest differentiator and it sits mid-list as "HMRC & IHT Preparation". Competitors charge a heavy premium for exactly this.

**What competitors do:**
- Octopus: Grant with IHT forms is a **£1,290** tier (vs £585 without)
- KCT: IHT400 for a non-taxable estate is a **+£1,200** add-on
- Octopus leads its service copy with "Work out what inheritance tax you need to pay... help you submit"

**Suggestions:**
- Promote IHT to a hero or near-hero message. Draft:
  > **Your IHT forms, completed and included.** Others charge £1,290 for inheritance tax forms. We complete them from the documents you upload, included in your £297.
- Add a dedicated IHT section explaining what's covered (IHT205/IHT400 as applicable, thresholds, transferable nil-rate band) so it reads as substantive, not a checkbox.

---

## 4. Anchor the price advantage properly

**Gap:** "Skip £1,000+ in legal fees" undersells the real saving and gives no anchor. The actual market makes £297 look extraordinary.

**Market prices (2026, from the harness and competitor sites):**

| Provider | Service | Price |
|---|---|---|
| GOV.UK DIY | Grant only, no help | ~£316 (fee only) |
| Budget online (ClearLegacy etc.) | Grant only, thin service | £195–£1,500 |
| Octopus Legacy | Grant, no IHT | £585 |
| Octopus Legacy | Grant **with IHT forms** | £1,290 |
| Farewill | Complete administration | from £2,750 |
| Kings Court Trust | Grant of Probate | £1,995 |
| Co-op / banks | Percentage of estate | ~2–4% (£6k–£12k on a £300k estate) |
| **ProbateSwift** | **Guided self-serve + IHT forms** | **£297** |

**Suggestions:**
- Replace "£1,000+" with a concrete contrast. Draft:
  > **£297, all in.** Not £1,290 for IHT forms. Not £2,750 for full administration. Not 2 to 4% of the estate. One flat fee, paid only when you submit.
- Add a comparison row or simple table on the page. The gap does the selling.
- Make the percentage-fee story explicit somewhere: "A bank could charge £8,000 to administer a simple estate. We charge £297." That single line is your strongest acquisition message per the harness.

---

## 5. Concrete timelines

**Gap:** "Cutting weeks off the timeline" is vague. Competitors quantify every stage, which reads as competence.

**What competitors do:**
- Farewill: Value the estate (1 week to 2 months), Get probate (1 to 3 months), Sort the estate (3 to 6 months)

**Suggestions:**
- Show a concrete journey with real durations: upload (minutes), forms auto-filled and reviewed (same day), submission, grant from the Registry (state current ~8 to 12 weeks, as Farewill does).
- Replace "cutting weeks off" with the specific thing you remove: "Forms that take most people days, done in an afternoon."

---

## 6. Scope honesty: what's included and what isn't

**Gap:** Competitors are explicit about exclusions and boundaries. ProbateSwift's copy doesn't draw the line, which paradoxically reads as less trustworthy and risks refunds.

**What competitors do:**
- Farewill: "What's not included: third-party fees like a stock broker or legal fees to sell a property"
- KCT: clear add-on list; Octopus: "additional services and costs" section

**Suggestions:**
- Add a short "What's included / What's not" block to the pricing section.
- Add an honest "When you might still need a solicitor" line: disputed wills, contested estates, complex trusts, overseas assets. Naming this protects you and makes the core promise more believable.

---

## 7. Amplify free-until-submit (a genuine edge nobody else has)

**Gap:** You have a real differentiator that every competitor lacks, and it's understated. Theirs is "no upfront, paid from the estate". Yours is "use the whole thing free, pay only if you choose to submit".

**Suggestions:**
- Make it a headline-level promise, not a footnote. Draft:
  > **Use the entire service free. Pay £297 only when you submit.** Fill everything in, see your completed forms, change your mind at any point. No card needed to start.
- This pairs with the validation message in area 2: free to try, checked before you commit, only pay when ready.

---

## 8. FAQ depth (SEO and reassurance)

**Gap:** Your FAQ is solid but thinner than competitors', who use depth to rank and to reassure.

**What competitors do:**
- Octopus and Co-op cover bank thresholds (as high as £50k, as low as £5k), joint-tenancy survivorship, intestacy detail, who can apply, the £10k common threshold.

**Suggestions:**
- Expand FAQ to cover: bank/institution thresholds, joint vs sole ownership and survivorship, who can apply (executor vs administrator, bankruptcy, capacity), what IHT forms apply at what estate value.
- This doubles as SEO landing content. The "Do I need probate?" assessment tool is a strong front door; richer surrounding content will pull more search traffic into it.

---

## What's already working (keep, don't lose in the redesign)

- "Is probate required?" assessment as a front-door hook. Octopus and Co-op both run dedicated "do I need probate" funnels, so you're aligned. Keep it prominent.
- Free-until-submit and no-card-required. Strengthen, don't dilute.
- The upload to auto-fill to submit three-step clarity. Clean and easy to follow.

---
