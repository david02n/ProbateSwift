## Problem
Inheritance Tax reporting — the IHT400 and its supplementary schedules (IHT405 property, IHT403
gifts, etc.) — was designed for professional advisers, not bereaved family members. Applicants must
value assets correctly, find the right schedules, obtain an IHT reference before submitting, and
report gifts within 7 years.

## Pain
Who: any executor of an estate needing IHT reporting (a growing share — frozen thresholds, and from
Apr 2027 pensions count toward the estate, adding ~10,500 newly-liable estates/yr). How often: every
IHT-reportable estate. How badly: mistakes trigger HMRC penalties (up to £3,000), interest at ~7.75%
on tax paid late (due 6 months from death), weeks/months of delay when schedules are missing, and
real anxiety. Workaround today: pay a solicitor/accountant, or attempt it via generic GOV.UK guidance
and risk errors.

## Evidence
Multiple 2026 guides state the IHT400 "is not fit for purpose" for laypeople; common documented
mistakes: wrong valuations, missing IHT405/IHT403, submitting before obtaining the IHT reference.
HMRC penalties up to £3,000 and 7.75% interest cited. The 2027 pensions-into-IHT change is legislated
(Finance Act 2026), widening the affected population.
