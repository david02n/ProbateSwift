## Problem
Users can't reliably tell whether their estate is a safe self-serve case or one needing a specialist
(disputes, insolvency, complex trusts, foreign assets, contested estates, tricky IHT reliefs).

## Pain
Who: executors of borderline/complex estates. How often: the minority of cases, but high-stakes. How
badly: attempting self-serve on a genuinely complex estate risks personal liability, penalties, and
costly clean-up; over-caution sends simple estates to expensive firms unnecessarily. Workaround: pay
for a consultation to find out, or guess.

## Evidence
PRD's three-outcome design explicitly calls for triage-out; "one mistake can exceed solicitor fees"
is documented. Severity is clear; precise frequency/segmentation still needs user-data validation —
hence evidence: researched, not validated.
