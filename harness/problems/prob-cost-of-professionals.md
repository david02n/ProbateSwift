## Problem
The jump from the £300 DIY court fee to professional help is steep and often percentage-based, so
cost scales with estate value rather than work done.

## Pain
Who: executors of ordinary estates who feel out of their depth. How often: most who don't DIY. How
badly: banks/traditional firms charge 2–4% (£6,000–£12,000+ on a £300k estate); solicitor full admin
£3,000–£6,000 (£15,000+ with IHT/property/disputes). For a relatively simple estate this is widely
perceived as poor value. Workaround: risk DIY, or pay and resent it.

## Evidence
Documented fee ranges (2026): percentage 2–4%; grant-only £950–£1,500+VAT; full admin £3,000–£6,000;
£15,000+ for complex. DIY route = £300 court fee only. The gulf is the core market opening.
