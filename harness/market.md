## Market size & volume
~305,000 applications for grants of representation were made in 2025 (+2% on 2024); ~242,800
were probate-grant applications and ~239,100 probate grants were issued. Probate is ~81% of all
grants of representation. Volume is structurally rising with the ageing population — the addressable
event (a death requiring probate) recurs ~240k+ times a year in E&W alone.

## Channel & willingness to pay
- **DIY / direct (GOV.UK):** the application itself costs a **£300 court fee** (estates >£5,000;
  free at/below £5,000). ~80–93% of applications are now submitted **digitally**, showing strong
  comfort with an online journey. This is ProbateSwift's "free alternative" benchmark.
- **Online fixed-fee services:** typically **£500–£1,500** for a straightforward estate (e.g.
  Farewill "Essential" from £595 + registry fee; "Complete" from £1,500).
- **High-street solicitors:** grant-only **£950–£1,500 +VAT**; full estate administration
  **£3,000–£6,000**, exceeding **£15,000** for IHT/multi-property/disputed estates.
- **Banks & traditional firms:** **percentage** fees of **2–4% of estate value** — £6,000–£12,000+
  on a £300k estate. This percentage model is the clearest target to undercut with a flat fee.

The willingness-to-pay ladder is wide (£0 DIY → £15k+ full admin), and ProbateSwift's flat-fee
intent slots into the **£300–£1,500 gap** with a transparent, predictable price.

## Regulation & framework changes (the "why now")
- **IHT on pensions from 6 April 2027** (Finance Act 2026, Royal Assent 18 Mar 2026): most unused
  pension funds and death benefits fall into the estate for IHT. ~10,500 estates/yr become newly
  IHT-liable; before probate, personal representatives must report full value (incl. pensions) to
  HMRC and settle tax. This materially widens the population facing IHT paperwork — directly
  expanding the pain ProbateSwift addresses.
- Frozen IHT thresholds + rising asset/house values drag more estates toward IHT reporting.
- Probate is now overwhelmingly **digital**, and HMCTS turnaround has improved (avg ~5 weeks in
  2025, digital ~2 weeks), but **stopped applications** remain a major friction point (~21 weeks).

## Buyer
A grieving layperson in E&W (named executor / spouse / adult child) administering an estate, who
wants confidence and to avoid unnecessary solicitor cost. Secondary: probate providers and legal
professionals wanting a structured intake/automation front-end.
