# ProbateSwift — Competitor Pricing Landscape (E&W, June 2026)

Reference for pricing / positioning and the willingness-to-pay test. The **£300 probate registry fee
(+£16/copy) is on top of every paid option below** — none of these headline prices include it. VAT
treatment varies: Octopus/Farewill prices include VAT; most solicitor fees add 20%.

## Where ProbateSwift sits

The paid market splits into two clusters with a gap in between:

- **Cheap-and-thin (£195–£900):** grant-only form-filling. Octopus Legacy is the disruptor here — £585 *with IHT forms included* is the price/scope to beat.
- **Full-and-pricey (£1,500–£12k+):** done-for-you estate administration, human-led.

ProbateSwift's intended flat fee (£297 provisional) sits **below the entire paid market** and only above the ~£316 DIY route. The IHT-form completion you're building is priced by Octopus at **£1,290** — so the value you'd deliver is worth far more than £297 to the market.

## Read-through for the pricing decision (`dec-pricing-model`)

1. **The IHT-form benchmark is £1,290 (Octopus), not £585.** £585 is grant-only with *no* IHT forms. So "we fill your IHT forms, self-serve" has a lot of headroom — £297 likely underprices it badly. Strong case to test **£349–£599**.
2. **Two natural ProbateSwift tiers mirror Octopus's split:** a cheap/free path for no-IHT estates (vs their £585) and a paid tier for IHT-form estates (vs their £1,290). Your single-SKU decision could still hold, but the IHT case is where the willingness-to-pay clearly sits.
3. **Octopus is the benchmark, and watch their AI.** They bought NewLaw's probate team (May 2026) to scale with AI — the medium-term risk is they drop the £1,290 IHT price. Frame the WTP test against Octopus's tiers.
4. **The budget cluster (£195–£355) anchors the floor** for the no-IHT/grant-only case. Above that, you must visibly beat "form-filling-as-a-service".
5. **Percentage-fee firms remain the narrative villain** for marketing, even if they're not the head-to-head product comparison.
