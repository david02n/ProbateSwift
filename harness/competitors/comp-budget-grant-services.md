## Positioning
A cluster of low-cost, online, grant-only providers sitting just above the free DIY route. They take
your details and obtain the grant for a low flat fee, but are **not** end-to-end guided self-serve and
generally don't remove the IHT-form or estate-tracking pain. Examples: **ClearLegacy from £195**,
**UK Probate £355 / £750**, **Probate Bureau £750–£1,500 +VAT**.

## Pricing (2026)
Floor around **£195–£355** for the cheapest grant-only, rising to ~£1,500 for fuller grant services.
These define the **bottom of the paid market** — the prices ProbateSwift's flat fee will be measured
against by price-sensitive users.

## Strengths
Very cheap; simple; "cheaper than a solicitor, easier than DIY".

## Gaps ProbateSwift exploits
Thin service: form-filling-as-a-service, little guidance, no triage, no IHT completion, no dashboard,
no validation. ProbateSwift's heavily-guided self-serve journey is a clear step up — but these set the
**price anchor**: a ProbateSwift flat fee far above ~£355 needs to justify the gap on experience and
IHT/validation value, not just "cheaper than Farewill".
