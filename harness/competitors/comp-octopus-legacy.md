## Positioning
Venture-backed "plan for and through death" brand (wills, estate planning, probate, bereavement),
**SRA-regulated** (Octopus Legal Services), solicitor-led. The most direct DTC threat to ProbateSwift,
but its pricing is **tiered by IHT complexity**.

## Pricing (2026, verified from octopuslegacy.com/probate 2026-06-11)
All prices **include VAT** but **exclude** the £300 registry fee and £16 per grant copy:
- **Grant of Probate, no IHT forms: £585**
- **Grant of Probate, with IHT forms: £1,290** ← this is the IHT-completion offer, not £585
- **Full estate administration: from £2,730** (e.g. property to sell ≈£4,883; +stocks/IHT ≈£6,353; complex ≈£11,183)

Earlier note that £585 included IHT forms was **wrong** — IHT-form completion is the £1,290 tier.

## Strengths
Low headline price *with* IHT help included; strong brand and design; will/estate-planning funnel;
**scaling probate with AI** — acquired NewLaw's probate team (May 2026) for AI-assisted throughput.
Venture funding to spend on acquisition.

## Gaps ProbateSwift exploits / threat
Closest competitor to ProbateSwift's wedge, but human-assisted/solicitor-led, not self-serve. Crucially,
their **IHT-form completion is a £1,290 tier**, not £585 — so ProbateSwift's "we fill your IHT forms"
promise at a low flat fee has **clear room beneath them** (a self-serve £349–£499 sits well under £1,290).
The grant-only £585 is the floor to watch for the no-IHT case. Their **AI push** (NewLaw team, May 2026)
is the real medium-term risk: it could pull the IHT-tier price down. **Primary benchmark for pricing and
scope — but the gap is wider than first thought.**
